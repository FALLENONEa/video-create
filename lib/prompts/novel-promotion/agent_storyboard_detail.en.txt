Rules:
1. Keep panel order unchanged.
2. Keep source_text semantically aligned with input; do not rewrite story meaning.
3. video_prompt should be motion-ready and concrete.
4. Prefer age+gender wording in video_prompt when naming actors in camera directions.
5. Preserve every input `slot` when the character is stably positioned, and reflect it as a preferred anchor in refined description/video_prompt.
6. `slot` is not an absolute boundary. If the shot is clearly about movement, entry/exit, path traversal, transition space, temporary space, empty space, imagination, dream, memory, or abstract/non-literal space, you may remove `slot` or keep it without forcing a rigid static match.

8. Do not shorten, rewrite, summarize, or replace a provided `slot` phrase with a short token.
9. Return JSON array only.
10. ⚠️ JSON SAFETY: All quotation marks in text (""''「」 etc.) MUST be converted to corner brackets「」in JSON string values. NEVER use raw ASCII double quotes " inside string values.
11. first_last_frame_prompt MUST contain all four dynamic elements, none optional: (1) action continuation — what the subject does during the transition and how its pose changes; (2) camera motion — direction/speed/scale of the move (push/pull/pan/truck/boom/orbit); (3) spatial change — how framing and scale shift into the next shot; (4) ⭐ transition texture — how things MOVE during these seconds: particles/VFX (shattering runes, surging mist, scattering sparks, creeping cracks), lighting shifts (brightness and color-temperature drift), environmental response (dust lifting, air shimmering, ground rippling), and the subject's micro-dynamics (clothes fluttering, hair flying, muscles tensing, micro-expressions).
12. ⚠️ Texture detail must only describe how things MOVE, never static appearance — faces, outfits, and scene layout are already locked by the first/last frames; restating them makes the text fight the images and corrupts generation.
13. ⚠️ Camera rhythm: never chain consecutive large same-direction moves (no back-to-back fast push-ins, fast pull-outs, or big boom moves). Insert at least one static/micro-move every 3-4 shots for breathing room, and alternate fast and slow moves to avoid a dizzying all-fast sequence. This applies to both camera_move and first_last_frame_prompt.
